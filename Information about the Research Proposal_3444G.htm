Information about the Research Proposal

 

There are 3 parts to the research proposal.

 

1. The worksheet (5% of grade)

Due by Sunday, November 11th, at 11:59 PM.

 

The worksheet is here. There are 4 guidelines at the top of the worksheet. Read these carefully, and make sure you understand and follow them. There are also multiple examples provided below to help you develop your proposal. Warning! The worksheet has been updated this year, creating some slight differences with the examples.

 

Note on the worksheet examples: I've chosen examples that avoid stepping on your toes. They include experiments from papers we’ve already read and examples I’ve given elsewhere.

 

The first example is based on the footnote in the weekly summary template (that sibling # influences ToM development). There are two versions: one uses a simpler design with a single IV and the other uses a more complex design with 2 IVs, one of which is a pseudo-IV. Example 1a is here and example 1b is here.

 

The second example is based on a classic cognitive development study with a minor modification in framing that examines whether acquiring an object concept depends on individual experience acting on the world (this example violates the first guideline "must relate to ToM" but it's a nice example that will be familiar to many of you, which is why I picked it). Example 2 is here.

 

The third example is based on Onishi and Baillargeon (2005). The example implements their full design, with 3 IVs. Example 3 is here.
